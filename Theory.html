Whats is SCSA?

SCSA is a novel algorithm for signal representation developed by professor Taous-Meriem Laleg-Kirati which is based on the discrete spectrum of a Schrodinger operator to analize signals as follows

Basic principle

Lets consider a signal represented by a real-valued function \(y(t)\), \(t \in \mathbb{R}\) such that for every t the function stays positive then we can define the Scrhodinger operator \(\mathcal{H}_h(y(t))\) as $$ \mathcal{H}_h(y)\psi(t) = -h^2\frac{d^2\psi(t)}{dt^2} - y(t)\psi(t) \quad, \quad \psi(t) \in H^2(\mathbb{R})$$

where \(H^2(\mathbb{R})\) denotes the sobolev space of order 2. Fort \(\lambda \leq 0\), we denote \(N_h(\lambda; y)\), the number of eigenvalues of the operator \(\mathcal{H}_h(y)\) below \(\lambda\). It has been proven that there is a non-zero finite number \(N_h = N_h(0:y)\). We denote \(-\kappa_{nh}^2\) the negative eigenvalues of \(\mathcal{H}_h(y)\) with \(\kappa_{nh} > 0\) and \(\kappa_{1h} > \kappa_{2h} > \cdots > \kappa_{nh}\), \(n=1,...,N_h\). Let \(\psi_{nh}\), \(n=1,...,N_h\) be the associated \(L^2\)-normalized eigenfunctions.

With the aformentioned we can then represent the signal \(y(t)\) with the discrete spectrum of \(\mathcal{H}_h(y(t))\) as follows $$y_h(t) = 4h\sum_{n=1}^{N_h} \kappa_{nh} \psi_{nh}^2(t), \quad t \in \mathbb{R}$$
